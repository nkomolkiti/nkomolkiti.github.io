When most people think about verbal abuse, they tend to summon up images of someone screaming and yelling. But while that's true in some households, it isn't always. — Peg Streep, writer

Sometimes what we intend to say doesn't come out the way we want it, especially when talking to children, as that can already easily miscommunicate. A normal statement or certain reactions, like rolling eyes, might turn into a quiet form of verbal abuse if used incorrectly and repetitively. When kids get mad, they get really mad. And parents, despite their best intentions, get mad too and often react without thinking clearly. Check out helpful phrases below. They are not absolute, nor can they be used with every child. They help parents be aware of the importance of words.
